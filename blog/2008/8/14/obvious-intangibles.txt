<p><a href="http://sethgodin.typepad.com/seths_blog">Seth Godin</a> has just put up a fantastic post on <a href="http://sethgodin.typepad.com/seths_blog/2008/08/the-intangibles.html">The intangibles</a> &#8212; ways to set your product or service apart.</p>
<p>Many of them read as <em>common-sense</em> or <em>obvious</em>, but like most <em>common-sense</em> and <em>obvious</em> advice, it is rarely actually practiced; often the advice is even actively dismissed with an annoyed &#8216;Of course we do that!&#8217; &#8212; only you do not do that, and you are fooling yourself by saying so.</p>
<p>Take stock of what you are doing, and ask yourself if you are really doing the <em>common-sense</em> and <em>obvious</em> things.</p>
